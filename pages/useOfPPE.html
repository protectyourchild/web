Gloves


When handling a contaminated equipment, body fluids, blood, secretions or excretions, gloves should be used. It is used to provide safety to all individuals against various pathogenic microorganisms. But sometimes gloves can be used improperly and failure to remove them at the correct time that leads to the increased risk of transmitting infections to the exposed individual.


Sterile Gloves vs Non-Sterile Gloves/ Clean Gloves


Sterile Gloves are used when there is a surgical procedure or vaginal procedure and any radiological procedure.


Non-Sterile Gloves/ Clean Gloves are used when it has an exposure to any blood or body fluid, non-intact skin, secretions, excretions, mucous membranes, or any equipment that has been contaminated.

HOW TO USE THE GLOVES?

  • Gloves should be put on immediately and remove after the procedure.
  • Hands should be sterilized and dried before putting the gloves. Apply waterproof dressings to the cuts and broken skin.
  • Gloves are single-use and it is not allowed to washed or reuse.
  • When the gloves are being contaminated, it should be changed.
  • After removing the gloves, hands should be washed and dried or disinfecting using alcohol or hand rub.
  • Limit the potential for “touch contamination” by unnecessary contact with the face/environmental surfaces when wearing gloves that may have been contaminated.

HOW TO KEEP THE GLOVES?

  • Gloves should be placed on their original packaging in a clean dry surface.
  • Healthcare workers should not put their gloves in the pockets of their clothes/ uniforms.

Masks


Face masks are used for preventing the spread of diseases. It limits the spread of the germs. It covers the nose and mouth. It is loose-fitting and has ear loops or ties or bands at the back of the head. Individuals should use a face mask approved by the FDA. It reduces the number of germs that the sick person and can protect the other people from becoming sick. Individuals also protected by wearing face masks from splashes of body fluids.

But despite of these studies, handwashing remains as the best preventive way in fighting illnesses.



TYPES OF FACE MASK

  • Surgical Masks
    • These are one of the types of that we can use to protect us in acquiring any diseases such as respiratory illnesses. It prevents us from the large droplets of contaminated body fluids and the splashes and spray from other people when they are sneezing or coughing.
    • But not all surgical masks that we can found in the local drugstores can filter viruses. So, experts recommend special masks with fine mesh that can capture microorganisms. It should be worn properly to have a guarantee that it will work.
    • Masks worn over the face does not give an assurance that it will protect us from getting any airborne virus particles.
  • Respirators
    • Another type of masks that is also called as “N95 Respirator Masks”.
    • N95 Face Masks are made to protect us from both small and large particles of viruses.
    • It can filter 95 percent of airborne particles which its name came from.
    • It is also used often in painting or handling any toxic materials.
    • Respirators are selected to fit the face. Make sure that it perfectly seals the face to avoid airborne viruses from entering.
    • Above all, respirators are more effective in preventing flu virus than regular face masks.

WHEN SHOULD A FACE MASK BE WORN?

  • Face mask should be worn by those people who have sick like cough, cold, and when there are sneezing when they are in a crowded place.
  • Face mask should protect the other people from carrying any diseases that is contagious.
  • Disposable face masks should be discarded after the single use.
  • When the face mask becomes moist, it should be replacing immediately.
  • Healthcare facilities have specific guidelines when should healthcare workers wear face masks.

HOW TO PUT ON A FACE MASK?

  • Practice proper hand hygiene. Hands should be wash by soap and water or use Alcohols or Hand Sanitizers before touching the face masks.
  • Remove mask from the box and make sure there are no evidence of holes or tears in either side of the mask
  • Recognize which side of the mask is on the top. The side of the mask that has a stiff bendable edge is the top and is meant to mold to the shape of the nose.
  • Determine which side of the mask is on the front. The colored side of the mask is usually the front and should face away from the individual while the white side touches the face of the person.
  • For the face mask with ear loops, hold the mask by the ear loops. Place a loop around each ear. For the face mask with ties, bring the mask to your nose level and place the ties over the crown of the head and secure with a bow. For the face mask with bands, hold the mask in your hand with the nosepiece or top of the mask at fingertips, allowing the headbands to hang freely below hands, bring the mask to your nose level and pull the top strap over your head so that it rests over the crown of your head, and pull the bottom strap over your head so that it rests at the nape of your neck.
  • Mold or pinch the stiff edge to the shape of the nose.
  • If using a face mask with ties: then take the bottom ties, one in each hand, and secure with a bow at the nape of your neck.
  • Pull the bottom of the mask over your mouth and chin.

HOW TO REMOVE A FACE MASK?

  • Practice proper hand hygiene. Hands should be wash by soap and water or use Alcohols and Hand Sanitizers before touching the face masks.
  • For the face mask with ear loops, hold both of the ear loops and gently lift and remove the mask. For the face mask with ties, untie the bottom bow first then untie the top bow and pull the mask away from you as the ties are loosened. For the face mask with bands
  • Dispose the used face mask into the designated trash bin.
  • Wash your hands thoroughly.

Sequence of Putting On and Removing PPE


The type of PPE used is based on the type of exposure and not all items of PPE will be required. Consider the use of these items under standard infection control precautions or if there are other routes of transmission.



SEQUENCE OF PUTTING ON PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)


Perform hand hygiene or Hand rubbing every time that you will be putting the PPE.

  1. Gown/Fluid Repellent Coverall
    • Fully cover torso neck to knees, arms to end wrist and wrap around the back. Fasten at the back.
  2. Surgical Mask (or respirator)
    • Secure ties or elastic bands at middle of head and neck. Fit flexible band to nose bridge. Fit snug to face and below chin. Fit/check respirator if being worn.
  3. Eye Protection (Goggles or Face shield)
    • Place over face and eyes and adjust to fit.
  4. Gloves
    • Select according to hand size. Extend to cover wrist.

SEQUENCE OF REMOVING PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)

  1. Gloves
    • Outside of gloves are contaminated. Grasp the outside of the glove with the opposite gloved hand; peel off.
    • Hold the removed glove in the gloved hand. Slide the fingers of the ungloved hand under the remained glove at the wrist. Peel the second glove off over the first glove. Discard it into the appropriate lined trash bin.
  2. Gown/Fluid Repellent Coverall
    • The front and the sleeves are contaminated. Unfasten neck, then waist ties.
    • Remove using a peeling motion; pull gown/fluid repellent coverall from each shoulder towards the same hand.
    • The gown/fluid repellent coverall will turn inside out. Hold removed gown/fluid repellent coverall away from body, roll into a bundle and discard into an appropriate lined waste bin.
  3. Eye protection (Goggles or face shield)
    • Outside of goggles or face shield are contaminated. Handle only by the headband or the sides. Discard into a lined waste bin or place into a receptacle for decontamination.
  4. Surgical Mask (for respiratory)
    • Do not touch the front of the mask/respirator are contaminated. Unfasten the ties. First, the bottom, then the top. Pull away from the face without touching the front of mask/respirator. Discard disposable items into an appropriate lined waste bin. For reusable respirator place in designated receptacle for processing/decontamination.

Perform hand hygiene immediately after the removal of the PPE. All PPE should be removed before leaving the place and should be discard into the appropriate waste bin.
